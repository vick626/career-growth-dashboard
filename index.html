Key findings
Tenure drives growth scores: Over-2-year staff score 3.8/4 on career growth — nearly double the under-6-month cohort (2.2). The longer you stay, the more OPay delivers on growth.
Leavers rate growth higher: Ex-employees average 3.4/4 on career growth vs 3.0 for current staff — suggesting growth potential isn't always visible from inside.
Skills are the strongest positive: 86% report improvement regardless of tenure or leaver status. OPay consistently builds capability even when other metrics are mixed.
Long-term career help is split: 68% say OPay helped their career (significantly + somewhat), but 32% say "not really" or "not at all" — pointing to an experience gap across roles or departments.
